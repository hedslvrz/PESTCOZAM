Soil Poisoning Treatment

Soil poisoning is a preventive pest control method designed to protect buildings and structures from subterranean termite infestations. This treatment involves applying a specialized termiticide to the soil before or after construction, creating a chemical barrier that repels and eliminates termites. It is an essential step in termite-proofing properties, ensuring long-term protection against structural damage.

Service Process

  1. Site Inspection: Our experts assess the area to identify potential termite threats and determine the appropriate treatment plan.
  2. Soil Preparation: The ground is prepared by loosening the soil around the foundation, trenches, or designated treatment zones.
  3. Termiticide Application: A high-quality, long-lasting termiticide is evenly applied to the soil, forming a protective barrier.
  4. Absorption & Drying: The treated soil is allowed to absorb the chemical, ensuring deep penetration for maximum effectiveness.
  5. Post-Treatment Inspection: Our team conducts a final check to ensure proper application and effectiveness.

Target Pests

  • Subterranean termites
  • Other soil-dwelling insects that may cause property damage

Safety Measures

  • We use government-approved, eco-friendly termiticides that are safe for humans, pets, and the environment.
  • Clients are advised to stay away from the treated area until the termiticide fully dries, typically within a few hours.

Estimated Time

  • Pre-Construction Treatment: 2–5 hours, depending on the property size.
  • Post-Construction Treatment: 3–6 hours, including drilling and soil injection.

Customer Preparation

  • Clear the treatment area of any obstacles, such as debris or plants.
  • Inform our team about underground utilities or water pipelines to prevent accidental damage.
